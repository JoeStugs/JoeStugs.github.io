About My Aviation Journy

In high school I became a member of the Civil Air Patrol, the civilian auxiliary branch of the U.S. Air Force.  As a cadet, I was able to go on three orientation flights where I was introduced to the world of planes from the front seat.  Through CAP I was also able to visit Flight Safety International, a simulator training center for commercial pilots.  Through getting to try out their simulators and talk to their instructors that I realized wanted to pursue aviation as a career.

That is why I decided to go to Bob Jones University and minor in Aviation.  I now have my private pilot's license with an instrument rating.  I am working on getting my commercial license and plan on becoming a flight instructor.  From there who knows where I will end up.  There are lots of opportunities for an experienced pilot.
